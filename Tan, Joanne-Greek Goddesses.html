Greek Goddesses

  • Aphrodite

    • Goddess of Love and Beauty

    The birth of Aphrodite is not very clear. Some say that Zeus and Dione gave birth to Aphrodite. Other's say that she was born from Uranus's genitals when Cronus castrated them and threw them into the sea.
    Zeus forced Aphrodite to married Hephaestus (known as the only "ugly" god) so that the other gods would stop fighting for her hand in marriage.
    Roman name: Venus
  • Athena

    • Goddess of Wisdom

    Athena is also the goddess of reason, purity, strategy and war. Unlike other gods, she was born from Zeus's head and steped out fully clothed in armor.
    Athena is described as courageous and fierce in battle. Since she was Zeus's favourite child, he gave her permission to use his weapons as well.
    Roman name: Minerva
  • Artemis

    • Goddess of the Hunt

    Artemis is the twin sister of Apollo and daughter of Zeus and Leto. She is also the goddess of childbirth, virginity, nature, and the moon.
    Roman name: Diana
  • Hera

    • Goddess of Marriage and Family

    Hera is Zeus's wife and sister. In the beginning, Zeus tried to court her, but was not successful. He then decided to trick Hera. He transformed into a tousled cuckoo. When Hera held the cuckoo close, Zeus transformed back into his regular form. He took advantage of her suprise and raped her. To cover her embarassment and shame, she married Zeus. This may be the primary reason why they do not get along.
    Roman name: Juno
  • Hestia

    • Goddess of the Hearth

    Hestia is also the goddess of family and domestic life. She is the sister of Zeus. Hestia was orginally a part of the Olympians until she was replaced by Dionysus. She is not greatly worshipped, but her Roman counter-part is.
    Roman name: Vesta
